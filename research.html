Visual primer

Before the numbers, see the loop that links AI, heat, and water.

1. Compute creates heat

As AI servers work harder, heat builds fast. That heat cannot stay in the hardware.

2. Cooling removes that heat

Cooling systems push the heat away using air, liquid, or evaporation, each with different tradeoffs.

3. Water systems feel the strain

Once cooling scales across many facilities, hidden water demand becomes a public issue, not just a technical one.

Core framing

Why the phrase "water bankruptcy" changes the conversation.

Beyond water stress

The research frames water bankruptcy as persistent over-withdrawal beyond what nature can safely replenish.

Why AI belongs here

AI is often judged only by energy use or emissions, yet the hardware behind it also depends on water-intensive thermal management.

Why awareness matters

Public understanding is still lagging behind technical expansion. That gap is where this project can create impact.

Research takeaways

What visitors should understand from the evidence

01

Training large models is resource intensive

The underlying research cited here points to roughly 700,000 liters of clean freshwater evaporated during GPT-3 training in certain data center conditions.

02

Inference adds up at global scale

A single user session may feel small, but millions of prompts and ongoing inference workloads create system-wide pressure.

03

Thermodynamics drives the issue

Computing creates heat. Cooling systems remove that heat through air, water, and specialized liquids, each carrying tradeoffs.
