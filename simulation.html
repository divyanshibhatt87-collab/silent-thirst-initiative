One chat times the population

If one longer chatbot session uses up to 500 milliliters of water, scale it across the world.

Illustrative public calculation

500 mL x 8.2 billion people

Assumption used here: one longer chatbot session for each person on Earth.

500 mL x 8,200,000,000
= 4,100,000,000,000 mL
= 4,100,000,000 liters
= 4.1 million cubic meters of water

Concern signal

At global scale, even one seemingly simple AI interaction per person becomes a massive water event.

The point is not that every person will do exactly one chatbot session. The point is that digital actions do not stay digital for long. Once usage scales across populations, the water cost becomes a public-resource issue.

Human-scale comparison

Using a basic benchmark of 50 liters per person per day for essential daily needs:

500 mL x 8.2 billion people = 4.1 million m^3 of water
4.1 billion liters / 50 liters per person per day = 82 million person-days
Enough for 100,000 people for about 820 days
Enough for a city of 250,000 people for about 328 days

4.1 million cubic meters of water could sustain 82 million person-days of essential daily water use. That is enough for 100,000 people for about 820 days, or a city of 250,000 people for nearly a year. When AI use scales globally, the hidden water cost stops being abstract. It becomes a question of whose water is being consumed, and for what.

The simulation is designed to make invisible infrastructure understandable to the public.
